Finding Real Happiness

It was yesterday evening around four, when I first encountered her on the street way back home from my institute. I was walking on a damp pavement to the nearer bus stop. My head felt heavy, filled with half-solved equations.


Then, I heard it - a tiny, rhythmic mew coming from behind a motorbike parked in front of a small garland shop. I paused for a moment, and found her standing with a gorgeous white fur along with orange and black patches. The instant I saw her, I could confirm, she is a Calico. Calicos are usually more brilliant, playful and vocal than other breeds. They can talk back to us. Her belly seemed bulged, her eyes were filled with hunger and hope. I mewed back at her. She didn't run. Instead, she walked right up to me with a loud husky mew. From the tone of her voice, I could sense that she had been starving for days. My heart sank the moment I had this realisation as her small body seemed fragile due to malnutrition.


I was so flustered as I didn't have anything to feed her. She kept following me as if she had known me all her life. Animals are pure and so are their trust on humans. I too wanted to take her with me to my home. Unfortunately, it's not possible as I already have 8 cats and a dog at my home.


I couldn't move a step after witnessing that tiny starving soul. My head felt heavy and felt like it would blast any moment as multiple thoughts with multiple emotions kept running all at once. Like,"I have huge bags of cat foods at home, but I don't have some now" with a regret; "What to feed her and how to feed her?" with a confusion; "There are no supermarkets or pet shops nearby" felt helpless; "What are the healthier alternatives I could feed her? Either curd, milk or boiled egg?"


I could not find any shops nearby that sells any of these. I searched nearby places restlessly before I could lose the sight of the cat.Then came an idea! The Grocery app. I added few cat mousse to the cart and rushed to locate the cat only to find out she had left already. This is the moment I felt like a stupid as I failed to find anything to feed her quickly.


I waited there for few more minutes, continuosly mewing to call her back. She never appeared. Then, I asked the garland shop owner about her. He said, the pregnant cat was a stray and was raised by the nearby temple. She used to sleep hiding under the table in the shop, hiding from other stray dogs.


On my way back home, the air felt heavy and the path felt lonely. I could not sleep the entire night thinking about the fact, she and the tiny souls inside her tummy are starving. Immediately, I packed some cat food and hid it inside my bag. The next day, at 2 PM, one of my classes at the institute got delayed for an hour. I was disappointed for the fact that I would be late to home. Meanwhile, I stepped out to have a quick snack as I didn't bring lunch for the day.


To my surprise, I found her again at the same spot. I ran towards her, fed her the cat food I had and gave the remaining to the shop owner, reminding her to feed the cat later. The shop owner with a puzzled face, asked me whether the cat will eat this. The next moment, she couldn't believe her eyes seeing the cat gobbling up all the cat food. She was amazed to see this. After having her meal, the cat went back to sleep peacefully in her space under the table. I made a request to the shop owner to keep a bowl of fresh water next to her. Then, I promised to bring more cat food the next day, thanked her and left for the day.


I walked the rest of the way home with a lighter step. The silence of the street was no longer lonely; it was peaceful. Yes! This was the day of my life, when I genuinely felt happy.


I had set out to learn math, but a stray cat had taught me something much more meaningful: Sometimes, the best way to heal your own heart is to notice a smaller one beating right next to you.
