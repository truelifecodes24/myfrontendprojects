Finding Real Happiness


An Unexpected Encounter With An Hungry Calico

It was yesterday evening around four, when I first encountered her on the street while returning home from my institute. I was walking along a damp pavement toward the nearby bus stop, my mind exhausted with half-solved equations and unfinished thoughts. Suddenly, I heard a tiny rhythmic mew coming from behind a motorbike parked near a small garland shop. I paused and found her there — a beautiful calico cat with white fur mixed with orange and black patches. The moment I saw her, I recognized her breed. Calicos are known to be playful, intelligent, and vocal, almost as if they can speak back to humans.

A Tiny Cry on a Damp Evening

Her belly looked swollen, and her eyes carried both hunger and hope. I mewed softly at her, expecting her to run away, but instead she walked right up to me with a loud husky cry. From the tone of her voice, I could sense she had been starving for days. Her tiny body looked weak and fragile from malnutrition, and my heart sank at the realization. I desperately wanted to feed her, but I had nothing with me. She kept following me as though she had known me forever. Animals are pure, and so is the trust they place in humans.

Feeling Helpless

I wanted to take her home, but it was impossible because I already had eight cats and a dog. Still, I could not bring myself to walk away after seeing such a starving creature. My thoughts raced endlessly. I regretted not carrying cat food with me even though I had plenty at home. I wondered what I could feed her — milk, curd, or a boiled egg — but there were no nearby shops selling anything suitable. I searched restlessly, afraid of losing sight of her. Then suddenly, an idea struck me: I could order cat food through a grocery app. Losing Sight of Her

I quickly added cat mousse to the cart and rushed back to where I had seen her, only to realize she had disappeared. In that moment, I felt foolish and deeply disappointed in myself for not acting fast enough. I waited there for several minutes, continuously mewing in the hope that she would return, but she never did. Finally, I asked the garland shop owner about her. He explained that the pregnant cat was a stray cared for by the nearby temple. She often slept beneath the table in the shop to protect herself from stray dogs.

A Night Filled with Worry

As I walked home, the evening felt unusually heavy and lonely. I could not stop thinking about her and the tiny lives growing inside her starving body. That entire night, sleep escaped me. The image of the hungry cat remained fixed in my mind. Early the next morning, I packed cat food into my bag before leaving for my institute, determined not to miss another chance to help her.

Meeting Her Again

The next day around 2 PM, one of my classes was unexpectedly delayed for an hour. Disappointed that I would reach home late, I stepped outside to grab a quick snack since I had not brought lunch. To my surprise, I saw her again at the exact same spot. Without hesitation, I ran toward her and fed her the cat food I had brought. I handed the remaining packets to the shop owner and requested him to feed her later as well.

A Small Act of Kindness

The shop owner looked puzzled and asked whether the cat would actually eat packaged cat food. Moments later, both of us watched in amazement as she eagerly gobbled it up. After finishing her meal, the cat peacefully returned to her little space beneath the table and curled up to rest. Seeing her finally relaxed and satisfied filled my heart with relief. Before leaving, I requested the shop owner to keep a bowl of fresh water beside her. I also promised to bring more cat food the following day.

Discovering Real Happiness

As I walked home that evening, my steps felt lighter than before. The silence of the street no longer seemed lonely; instead, it felt calm and peaceful. That day, I realized what genuine happiness truly means. I had left home to study mathematics, but a stray cat ended up teaching me something far more meaningful: sometimes, the best way to heal your own heart is by caring for another living soul that needs kindness the most.
